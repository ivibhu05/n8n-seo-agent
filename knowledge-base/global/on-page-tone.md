# On-Page Content Tone Guide

## Definition
On-page content lives on our own website. The brand is speaking directly to the reader. This is our house — we own the voice here.

## Core Tone Directive
**First person. We are the expert. We are the helper. The brand speaks.**

## Pronouns
- Brand → "we", "our", "us"
- Reader → "you", "your"
- NEVER refer to the brand in third person ("GryNow offers..." → "We offer...")

## Voice
- **Confident**: We know what we're talking about. We don't hedge unnecessarily.
- **Direct**: No beating around the bush. Tell them what they need to know.
- **Empathetic**: We understand what the reader is going through before we sell them anything.
- **Action-oriented**: Every piece moves toward something the reader should feel or do.

## What this sounds like
- "We help brands connect with the right creators — faster, smarter, at scale."
- "At GryNow, we've seen thousands of campaigns. Here's what actually works."
- "Our platform gives you everything you need to run a campaign from discovery to reporting."
- "Whether you're a startup running your first campaign or an enterprise brand scaling globally, we've built GryNow for you."

## What this does NOT sound like
- "GryNow is a platform that helps..." (third person — wrong for on-page)
- "Users of GryNow can..." (wrong — say "you can")
- "One can use the dashboard to..." (stiff, passive, wrong)

## Emotional register
- On-page content can and should create an emotional connection
- For B2B on-page: the emotion is confidence, trust, ambition
- For creator-facing on-page: the emotion is excitement, belonging, opportunity

## CTAs on on-page content
- Specific and action-first: "Start your first campaign", "Sign up free", "See pricing"
- Match CTA to where the reader is in the funnel:
  - Top-funnel blog reader → soft CTA ("Explore how GryNow works")
  - Bottom-funnel page visitor → hard CTA ("Get started today")

## Vocabulary watch for on-page
- Avoid "the platform" — say "we" or name a specific feature
- Avoid "users" — say "you", "our customers", "brands", "creators"
- Lead with outcomes, not process: "Drive real ROI" before "Using our matching algorithm"
