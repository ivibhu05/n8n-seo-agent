# Off-Page Content Tone Guide

## Definition

Off-page content is published on external platforms — guest posts, editorial articles, syndicated content, directories, or third-party blogs. The brand is being talked about, not talking itself.

## Core Tone Directive

**Third person. Authoritative. Journalistic. Informative. The brand is referenced, not narrated.**

## Pronouns

- Brand → refer by name ("GryNow", "MyWall") or as "the platform", "the company"
- NEVER use "we" or "our" in off-page content — it breaks the third-person editorial voice
- Reader → "you" is acceptable in how-to sections but use sparingly

## Voice

- **Authoritative**: Written as if by an industry expert or journalist covering the topic
- **Objective-leaning**: The brand can be positioned positively but must not read like an ad
- **Informative**: The primary job of off-page content is to educate, not sell
- **Credibility-first**: Support all claims with data or logic before referencing the brand

## What this sounds like

- "GryNow has become one of India's leading influencer marketing platforms, with thousands of brands and creators on its network."
- "The platform's approach to campaign matching has attracted [X] brands since its launch."
- "For brands looking to scale their influencer marketing efforts, tools like GryNow offer a structured alternative to manual outreach."
- "According to GryNow's data, campaigns that use micro-influencers see 3x higher engagement rates on average."

## What this does NOT sound like

- "We at GryNow believe..." (wrong — this is first person)
- "Our platform offers..." (wrong — never "our" in off-page)
- "GryNow is the BEST platform for..." (too promotional without substantiation)

## Structure pattern for off-page

1. Open with the topic/problem (not with the brand)
2. Establish credibility with industry context or data
3. Present solutions / approaches (brand can be one of them, naturally)
4. Reference the brand in context: "Platforms like GryNow have solved this by..."
5. Conclude with the reader's next step (can include a soft brand mention)

## Link placement in off-page

- Brand mention should naturally lead to a link — anchor text should be the brand name or a descriptive keyword, not generic phrases
- One to two brand links per piece is the ceiling for natural placement

## Tone balance: informative vs promotional

- 80% of the content should serve the reader's interest (information, education, entertainment)
- 20% or less can position or reference the brand
- If you're writing "GryNow is great because..." you've gone too promotional — frame it as "brands that use GryNow report..."

## Common off-page formats

- Guest blog posts on industry publications
- Listicles on third-party sites ("Top X tools for...")
- Press release style content (neutral, factual)
- Directory listings (concise, factual)
- Case study articles (data-backed, brand as solution)
