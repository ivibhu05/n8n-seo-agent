# Writing Guidelines — Global

## Structure Principles
- **Lead strong**: first paragraph must hook the reader and state the value
- **One idea per paragraph**: 3–4 sentences max per paragraph
- **Transitions matter**: each paragraph must flow logically to the next
- **Don't bury the lede**: most important information comes first, not last
- **Conclusion = action**: end with what the reader should do next

## Sentence & Language Rules
- Active voice always ("GryNow helps brands" not "brands are helped by GryNow")
- Vary sentence length — mix short punchy sentences with longer ones
- Max sentence length: 25 words
- Avoid nominalizations ("make a decision" → "decide")
- Avoid filler openers: "In today's world...", "It goes without saying...", "Needless to say..."
- Avoid adverbs that weaken: "very", "really", "quite", "basically", "literally"

## Readability Targets
- **Flesch Reading Ease**: aim for 60–70 (readable by a high schooler)
- **Paragraph length**: 3–5 lines on screen max (visual breathing room)
- **Use bullet points / numbered lists** when listing 3+ items
- **Bold key phrases** within paragraphs — not entire sentences
- **Use subheadings every 250–350 words**

## Content Types — Format Rules

### Blog Post
- Conversational but informed
- Tell a story or take a clear position — don't be neutral
- Use examples, analogies, case studies
- Structure: Intro → Problem/Context → Main content sections → Conclusion + CTA

### Web Page (On-Page Copy)
- Benefit-first, not feature-first ("You get X" not "We have X feature")
- Shorter sentences, punchier language
- Clear hierarchy: headline → subheadline → body → CTA
- Every section should move the reader toward one action

### Listicle
- Odd numbers perform better (7, 11, 15)
- Each list item: headline + 2–4 sentence explanation
- Items should be of similar depth — don't have 3 deep items and 10 shallow ones
- Intro must explain the selection criteria ("Here's what makes these stand out...")
- Conclusion must synthesize — don't just say "those were our picks"

## What to Avoid
- **Padding**: never repeat yourself to hit a word count target
- **Generic statistics**: "X% of marketers say..." only if cited and recent
- **Unsubstantiated superlatives**: "the best", "the most powerful" without proof
- **Walls of text**: break up every long section with formatting
- **Weak CTAs**: "learn more" → "Start your first campaign" / "See how it works"

## Writing Process (agent should follow this order)
1. Write the outline based on strategy brief
2. Write H2 sections in order
3. Write intro last (you need to know the full content to hook it right)
4. Write conclusion, then meta title + description
5. Do a keyword density check after writing — adjust naturally if over/under

## Quality Bar
Every piece should be something you'd be proud to read as a reader — not something that feels like it was written to rank. If it reads like SEO content, rewrite it until it reads like editorial content that also ranks.
